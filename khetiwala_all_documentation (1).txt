khetiwalaProject DescriptionProblem we solveIndia’s farmers lose up to 40% of their produce not because of bad farming, but because of poor timing and market mismatch. Farmers need timely, local, actionable advice — not complex dashboards. VibeCodeDocs closes that gap by combining real-time environmental data, market signals, and explainable AI recommendations into a lightweight, farmer-first app.Purpose & value propositionMaximise farmer income by recommending the optimal harvest window and best target market (net-profit aware).Minimise spoilage by predicting post-harvest risk and recommending ranked, cost-effective preservation actions.Build trust with transparent, evidence-based explanations for each recommendation.Make it accessible: voice-first, local language, and SMS support for basic Android phones.Target audienceSmall and medium farmers who primarily use basic Android phones.Aggregators / mandis / farmer producer organizations (FPOs) wanting supply forecasts.NGOs / government extension services seeking scalable advisory tech.Buyers and transporters who want validated, traceable listings.Core features (full list — described for documentation)Realtime crop listing & transactionsFarmers list produce quickly (crop, qty, harvest date, expected reserve price). Buyers browse, negotiate, and finalize transactions. Listings update in realtime and support cursor pagination for fast history browsing.AI price predictionDaily predicted mandi prices per crop-region derived from historical mandi data + weather + demand signals. Each prediction includes a confidence score and a short rationale: “price likely to rise because X; expected range Y–Z.”AI Crop-Switching Suggestion (game changer)Instead of only advising harvest timing, the system suggests profitable crop switches for the next season (e.g., “instead of soybean, sow chana”) based on price trends, rainfall forecast, and soil health. Each suggestion includes: expected margin lift, risk notes, and suggested government schemes to apply.Live Price Trend HeatmapSimple visual: last 30 days mandi-price trend per market. Color-coded: red = falling, green = rising. Tap a cell to see the numeric trend and recent drivers.Spoilage Risk Meter (visual indicator)One-tap meter: 🟢 Low / 🟡 Medium / 🔴 High. Driven by humidity, storage type, transit time, and temperature forecasts. Each risk level comes with prioritized mitigation steps.Farmer Voice Assistant ModeVoice Q&A: farmer asks (speech) in local language — the assistant replies with spoken, concise guidance. Example: “Gehu kab kaatna hai?” → voice answer with a 1–2 sentence recommendation and a one-line reason.Nearby Mandi Distance & Net-Profit OptimizationCalculates Net Profit = Price − Transport Cost; recommends the best market considering transport cost, distance, and time. Shows quick comparison: “Nearby Mandi → ₹A (net), Far Mandi → ₹B (net after transport).”Government Scheme RecommenderWhen a crop decision is made, the system suggests relevant subsidies, insurance, and scheme links (or SMS shortcodes) for quick enrollment. (Can start with curated/dummy data.)Storage Recommendation EngineWhen spoilage risk is non-low, system ranks preservation options (drying, cold storage, chemical preservatives, packaging) by cost and effectiveness, with estimated per-kg price impact.Demand Prediction ScoreSimple score (e.g., 8/10) for the next 7 days based on demand trend slope and buyer activity.Risk Alert NotificationsPush/SMS alerts for immediate threats: storms, predicted price crash, high spoilage risk. Alerts include a concise action (e.g., “Harvest within 48 hours / Move to nearby cold storage”).Farmer Profit Comparison SimulatorInteractive tool: input quantity & crop → shows “Sell Today → ₹X” vs “Sell After N days → ₹Y” and the difference. Helps build intuition and trust.Spoilage Prediction & Preservation ActionsPredicts spoilage likelihood and simulates how each preservation action changes spoiling probability and net-profit.Smart Logistics OptimizationSuggests cheapest / fastest transport routes and batching opportunities for multiple farmers (route estimates use maps and transport cost models).AI Advisory Assistant (explainable) — powered by ClaudeAnswer complex queries with reasoning: one-line recommendation + short rationale + data points used. Use an LLM as advisor but always surface supporting facts (price series, weather snippet, soil reading) so farmer sees why the model recommended something. We plan to use Anthropic’s Claude (or similar) as the reasoning engine for explainable text generation.Traceability & TransparencyEvery listing, advisory, and transaction is logged with the supporting data used for the recommendation so buyers and auditors can verify a decision trail.Fraud DetectionSimple anomaly detectors flag fake listings, duplicate accounts, or anomalous price spikes. Alerts go to admins and affected users.Analytics for Government / AggregatorsAggregated supply/demand dashboards for FPOs and regulators so they can plan procurement and interventions.UX principles & outputs for low-data literacy usersPlain language (local language first). designed to maximize Indian farmers' income by providing timely, localized, and explainable agricultural advice.1.2 Project GoalsMaximize farmer income by recommending the optimal harvest window and best net-profit-aware target market.Minimize post-harvest spoilage by predicting risk and recommending cost-effective preservation actions.Build trust through transparent, evidence-based, and explainable AI recommendations.Ensure accessibility via voice-first interface, local language support, and SMS fallback for basic Android users.1.3 Target AudienceSmall and medium farmers (1-10 notes, and suggested relevant government schemes.2.4 Live Price Trend Heatmap (Feature 4)Display a simple visual (last 30 days) of mandi price trends per market, color-coded (Red = falling, Green = rising).Tapping a cell displays the numeric trend and recent drivers.2.5 Spoilage Risk Meter (Feature 5)A one-tap visual indicator (🟢 Low / 🟡 Medium / 🔴 High) driven by humidity, storage type, transit time, and temperature forecasts.Each risk level must link to prioritized, cost-effective mitigation steps.2.6 Farmer Voice Assistant Mode (Feature 6)Farmer asks questions via speech in the 10)Simple score (e.g., 8/10) for the next 7 days based on demand trend slope and buyer activity on the platform.2.11 Risk Alert Notifications (Feature 11)Push/SMS alerts for immediate threats DatabaseTechnology: Supabase (PostgreSQL, Realtime, Auth, Storage)Justification:Rapid Prototyping & MVP: Supabase provides managed PostgreSQL, Authentication (essential for farmer/buyer security), Realtime subscriptions (critical for Feature 1: Realtime crop listing updates), and Storage in a single unified platform. This drastically reduces setup time for the hackathon/MVP phase.Scalability: PostgreSQL is robust and scales well for structured agricultural data (prices, user profiles, transaction logs).Realtime: Native support for web sockets ensures immediate updates for market listings (Feature 1) and alerts (Feature 11).Local Language Support: PostgreSQL handles Unicode strings natively, supporting local language data entry and storage.Technology: Python (FastAPI or Flask) for ML ServicesJustification:ML Ecosystem: Python is the industry standard for data science and machine learning (Price Prediction, Crop-Switching, Spoilage Modeling). FastAPI offers high performance suitable for serving these models via dedicated microservices.Data Science & AI LayerTechnology: Pandas, Scikit-learn, XGBoost (Initial Models)Justification:Price Prediction (Feature 2): XGBoost or simple time-series models (like Prophet/ARIMA initially) are excellent for structured time-series forecasting based on Agmarknet data and weather inputs.Crop-Switching (Feature 3, MVP Rule-Based): Initial logic will rely on heuristic scoring based on historical price averages and weather compatibility, easily implemented in Python/Pandas before complex ML.Spoilage Risk (Feature 5, 13): Rule-based heuristics initially, leveraging Python logic based on humidity/temp thresholds.Technology: Anthropic Claude (or similar LLM API)Justification:Explainable AI (Feature 15): Claude is chosen for its stated strengths in reasoning and explainability. It will process structured inputs (price trend, weather snippet, risk score) and generate human-readable, concise rationales in local languages.Voice Assistant (Feature 6): Used for generating synthesized, brief spoken answers based on pre-calculated results.Non-negotiable requirement: LLM output must always be anchored to structured, verifiable data points provided by the backend (Trust Layer).Frontend / Mobile ApplicationTechnology: Flutter (Recommended) or React NativeJustification:Cross-Platform Necessity: Target audience uses basic Android phones (Android 8.0+). A single codebase targeting Android ensures rapid deployment across low-end hardware.Performance (APK Size): Flutter offers better control over native compilation, helping keep the APK size well under the 200MB target, critical for low-storage devices.Offline Capabilities: Both frameworks support robust local storage solutions (SQLite/Hive) necessary for mandatory offline functionality (viewing advisories, drafting listings).Technology: Native Android (Kotlin) (Alternative/Augmentation)Justification:If Flutter performance proves insufficient on the lowest-spec target devices, targeted Kotlin modules can be built for high-priority features like Voice Q&A processing.Geographic Information Systems (GIS) & LogisticsTechnology: OpenRouteService (ORS) or Google Maps Platform (Static/Distance Matrix API)Justification:Net-Profit Optimization (Feature 7): ORS is often preferable for non-commercial or high-volume routing needs, offering open-source flexibility. Google Maps is an alternative for established distance calculation reliability.Cost Model Hybridization: APIs provide raw distance; the system must overlay regional freight rate cards (stored in PostgreSQL) to calculate realistic transport costs.Communication Fallback (SMS & Voice)Technology: Twilio or Regional SMS Gateway ProviderJustification:Reach Extension: Essential for Feature 11 (Risk Alerts) and providing basic advisory to users without smartphones or consistent internet access.Local Language Handling: The gateway must support Unicode/UTF-8 for rendering local Indian scripts correctly in SMS.Voice Synthesis (TTS): Twilio’s APIs or a dedicated Google Cloud Text-to-Speech service (tuned for regional accents) will power the spoken responses for the Voice Assistant.Data Acquisition & PipelinesTechnology: Cron Jobs / Supabase Scheduled FunctionsJustification:ETL: Nightly scheduled jobs are required to pull Agmarknet price data, process weather forecast updates, and store them in the PostgreSQL database.Data Granularity: Pipelines must normalize data across Mandi, Block, and District levels as defined in the data strategy.Security & TraceabilityTechnology: Supabase Auth, JWTsJustification:Authentication: Secure user management for farmers and buyers.Audit Trail (Feature 16): Structured logging of all model inputs, model versions, and LLM outputs (using generated audit schemas) must be stored immutably in PostgreSQL tables, accessible only by administrators/auditors.Technology: Device Fingerprinting/IP Tracking (Third-party SDK or Custom Logic)Justification:Fraud Detection (Feature 17): Used to detect multi-accounting or location discrepancies (IP mismatch), feeding into the rule-based anomaly detection system.Non-Functional Requirements SupportConstraint Mapping:APK Size/Performance: Achieved via Flutter optimization and server-side processing (AI, TTS).Offline Mode: Managed via local SQLite/Hive database synchronization strategy (Local-First Approach).Low-End Device Support: Mandates Android 8.0+ minimum SDK and highly optimized UI elements (large touch targets, minimal Key Relationships & IndexesUser Listings: `listings.farmer_id` -> `users.user_id` (One-to-Many)Price History: `mandi_prices` requires a unique index on (`mandi_id`, `crop_id`, `date`) for fast historical lookups.Advisory Traceability: `advisory_history.user_id` -> `users.user_id`. Document PurposeThis User Flow Document details the primary, high-value user journeys within the KhetiWala application, specifically focusing on the MVP scope: AI Price Prediction, Net-Profit Optimization, Spoilage Risk Meter, and the Voice Assistant interface. It maps interactions against the core UX principles designed for low-data literacy farmers.1.2. Core UX Principles AppliedThe following principles guide all user flows:• Voice-First Interaction: Assume user input via speech is the primary mode for complex queries.• Simplicity & Actionability: Every screen must lead to one clear, prioritized action or decision point.• Visual Hierarchy: Use color coding (Red/Yellow/Green) and meters over complex numerical tables for immediate comprehension.• Explainability (Trust): Every recommendation must have an easily expandable "Why this?" view showing 3 simple supporting facts.• Lightweight Design: Ensure flows are optimized for low bandwidth and older Android devices (API 26+).CORE USER FLOWS (MVP FOCUS)2.1. Flow 1: Daily Harvest/Sell Decision AdvisoryGoal: Farmer receives and acts upon the optimal time to sell the current crop to maximize net profit.Actors: Marginal / Small Farmer (P1)Entry Point: Home Screen / Notification AlertSteps and Interactions:1. Farmer opens KhetiWala (App loads quickly, showing cached status).2. System detects current crop status (pre-loaded or user confirms current crop: e.g., Tomatoes).3. System presents the primary decision card (Harvest/Sell Advice):• Visual Indicator: Spoilage Risk Meter (Feature 5) is displayed prominently (e.g., 🟡 Medium Risk).• Price Guidance: AI Price Prediction (Feature 2) shows next 5-day trend summary (e.g., "Price rising, wait 2 days for peak").4. Interaction A: Spoilage Risk Mitigation (If Risk is Yellow/Red):• Farmer taps Spoilage Risk Meter.• Screen displays ranked Storage Recommendation Engine options (Feature 9): e.g., [1. Drying (Cost: -₹0.1/kg), 2. Nearby Cold Storage (Cost: -₹1.5/kg)].• Farmer taps "Select Drying" (Single Action).• System updates expected Net Profit based on preservation cost impact.5. Interaction B: Net-Profit Optimization (Feature 7):• Farmer taps "Find Best Market."• Screen displays comparison table (Color Coded):• Nearby Mandi A: Price ₹22/kg | Transport ₹2/kg | Net ₹20/kg (Highlighted Green)• Far Mandi B: Price ₹24/kg | Transport ₹5/kg | Net ₹19/kg (Highlighted Yellow/Red)• System justifies selection in "Why this?" view: "Mandi A is closer, saving you ₹3/kg in transport."6. Final Action Prompt:• System presents clear action button: [Sell Today via Mandi A / Wait 2 Days] (Influenced by Price Trend).• If "Sell Today" is selected, system prompts listing creation (Flow 2).7. Trust Verification: Farmer taps expandable "Why this?" view to see 3 facts (e.g., Humidity rising, Mandi A profit margin, 3-day price forecast).Wireframe Description (Home Screen snippet):• Large Banner: Crop Status (Tomatoes, 50 Quintals)• Center Block: Spoilage Meter (Large 🟡 icon)• Below Meter: Price Trend Summary (Simple up arrow + 2 Days Wait)• Action Button: "Optimize Sale"2.2. Flow 2: Realtime Crop Listing & Transaction (Feature 1)Goal: Farmer quickly lists produce for sale, knowing the best market instantly.Actors: Progressive Medium Farmer (P2) / Aggregator-Linked Farmer (P3)Entry Point: Home Screen -> "List Produce" ButtonSteps and Interactions:1. Farmer taps "List Produce."2. Design Philosophy: Farmer-First SimplicityThe styling for Khetiwala is rooted in accessibility, trust, and actionable clarity. Our primary goal is to serve small and medium farmers with low data literacy on basic Android phones. We avoid glossy, modern, or data-heavy interfaces common in urban apps.2. UI/UX Principles (Accessibility and Trust)These principles directly address cross-persona barriers: Trust, Language/Literacy, and Network Constraints.5.1. Action Clarity (Single-Action Focus)Principle: Every screen should drive the user toward one primary, actionable outcome.Implementation: CTAs must be clearly delineated using Khetiwala Primary Green. Use labels like: [Sell Now], [Confirm Harvest], [Apply Scheme].De-emphasis: Secondary actions (e.g., [View Details], [Cancel]) should be text-only links or neutral buttons.5.2. Visual Cues Over NumericsPrinciple: Replace complex data analysis with immediate visual signals.Implementation:Spoilage Risk Meter (Feature 5): Must be the dominant element for risk assessment (🟢/🟡/🔴). Raw humidity/temp data belongs only in the expandable “Why this?” section.Price Trend Heatmap (Feature 4): Rely on Red/Green color coding first. Numeric value is secondary, revealed on tap.Confidence Score: Use visual meters (e.g., 3 filled stars out of 5) or color labels (High/Medium/Low) instead of raw percentages (Feature 17).5.3. Explainability Integration (Trust Layer)Principle: Every recommendation must have a visible, easy-to-access justification.Implementation: Use a universal "Why this?" affordance (e.g., an 'i' icon or an expandable chevron/down arrow) immediately adjacent to any AI recommendation (Price Prediction, Crop Switch, Advisory).Structure: Clicking this reveals the three curated facts derived from the inputs, using the smaller body text size (13–14sp).5.4. Voice First DesignPrinciple: The spoken response must precede or match the text presentation.Implementation: When the Voice Assistant is active, the primary feedback mechanism is audio. Text serves as confirmation/reference. Spoken responses must be brief (1–2 sentence max, as per Feature 6).5.5. Resource Constraint Management (Low-Data/Low-Power)Layout: Strict adherence to mobile-first, single-column layouts. Avoid nested scrolling views or complex tab bars that consume memory.Animation: Minimal, simple transitions only (e.g., fade-in/out). Avoid parallax, heavy motion, or large Lottie animations to preserve battery and CPU cycles.Feedback: Use immediate, lightweight feedback (e.g., a subtle vibration or a short toast message) instead of heavy loading spinners when performing local actions. For API waits, use persistent, labeled loading states.5.6. Net-Profit VisualizationPrinciple: Clarity in financial comparison is paramount (Feature 7).Implementation: Use a clean, side-by-side card layout for mandi comparison:Mandi Name	Price (₹/kg)	Transport Cost (₹/kg)	Net Profit (₹/kg)Nearby Mandi A	₹22	₹1.50	₹20.50 (Highlight Khetiwala Primary Green)Far Mandi B	₹24	₹4.00	₹20.00The Net Profit column must be visually weighted (bold text, slightly larger if possible) as it is the decision metric.
